What Changed

Feature Flow

Tap tempo: space key release feeds a sample-estimated tap tracker, smooths new gaps 50/50, and applies a seed grain to the existing Timer.

Master selection: shift-click on a hovered loop or loop take selects a master length and derives a seed that divides it exactly.

Visual feedback: loops now own a QuantisationModel child that draws radial gates as an additive scene-pass overlay.

NINJAM: BPM/BPI to sample interval conversion now uses the shared quantisation helper for local and remote tempo paths.

Rubber Duck Findings

Review Outcome

Fixed during review: free tap tempo was briefly treating the first completed tap interval as a master length. That would have pinned later taps to the first interval even before a real master loop was selected. The Scene tap path now leaves master length unset until shift-click chooses an actual loop or take.

Kept intentional: master-snapped tap timing chooses the nearest exact divisor of the master loop. That means a five-part split can win when it is closer than six; the test was corrected to document that rule.

Manual validation still useful: the visual overlay should be tried in the live app with actual 3D hover/select paths, because the current test suite validates the math and build integration rather than GPU pixels.
